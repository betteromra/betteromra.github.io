Hand-Painted Art Direction for a Narrative Exploration Game

Her Feather's Fire is a narrative-focused exploration game developed in a game jam. The player takes on the role of a crow navigating through a quiet village, observing subtle changes in the human world and gradually piecing together an environmental story. The experience blends story-driven design, point-and-click interaction, and side-scrolling exploration to create a reflective and atmospheric journey.

As the Artist on the project, my primary responsibility was establishing and executing the game's hand-painted visual direction. The goal was to create a cohesive aesthetic that supported the tone of mystery and quiet observation, while ensuring consistency across environments and interactive elements. I focused on building a visual language that felt organic and expressive, reinforcing the player's perspective as a curious observer in a human world.

Working within a very short production window required rapid iteration and close collaboration with designers and developers. I adapted assets quickly based on gameplay needs, ensuring readability in a side-scrolling format while preserving artistic intent. Despite the time constraints, the team maintained a unified direction, allowing the visual style to remain consistent across all scenes and interactions.

Overall, Her Feather's Fire was an exercise in fast-paced art direction and visual storytelling under constraint. The project strengthened my ability to define and execute a cohesive art style quickly, while supporting gameplay clarity and narrative tone.
